Ritzville Board Game

Frequently-Asked Questions

  • Do zero-point cards have to go on the bottom row?

    No. At the start of the game there will probably be some zero-point cards on the first or second row, and when you grow the town and take a card, the replacement card goes in the empty spot, even if it’s a zero-point card on another row.

  • Can the bottom row contain cards that are worth points?

    Yes. When you grow the town and take a card, the replacement card goes in the empty spot, even if it’s on the bottom row and has a point value. It’s only at the very start of the game where the bottom row contains only zero-point cards.

  • Can I take more than one chip of the same color if there aren’t enough colors left to take one of each?

    No. If there are fewer than three colors of chips available, you’ll have to take fewer than three chips.

  • Do I have to take three chips, even if I don’t go over my ten-chip limit?

    No, you can choose to take fewer chips.

  • I earned a +4 bonus but there are no +4 chips! What do I do?

    Take two +2 chips.

  • What if I need a +2 chip or +3 chip but there are no more available?

    You or any other player can make change by exchanging two +3 chips for three +2 chips or vice versa. If there are simply not enough bonus-point chips available, you can use coins or some other way to keep track of the bonus points you earn.

  • Can I use bonus-point chips to pay for cards and grow the town?

    No. Bonus-point chips cannot be used in place of chips of the correct color to grow the town. If you’re short a chip, you can always turn in three of any color chip as a wildcard chip.

  • Can I turn in six chips to use as two wildcard chips?

    Yes, you could, but consider whether it’s the best use of your chips. If it would win you the game, then go for it.

  • Can I trade cards with other players?

    No. Trading cards is not allowed.
